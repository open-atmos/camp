\doxysubsubsection{CAMP\+: SIMPOL.1 Phase-\/\+Transfer Reaction}
\hypertarget{camp_rxn__s_i_m_p_o_l_phase_transfer}{}\label{camp_rxn__s_i_m_p_o_l_phase_transfer}\index{CAMP: SIMPOL.1 Phase-\/Transfer Reaction@{CAMP: SIMPOL.1 Phase-\/Transfer Reaction}}
SIMPOL phase transfer reactions are based on the SIMPOL.\+1 model calculations of vapor pressure described by Pankow and Asher (2008) {\bfseries [Pankow2008]}.

Mass accomodation coefficients and condensation rate constants are calculated using the method of Ervans et al. (2003) {\bfseries [Ervens2003]} and references therein. Mass accomodation coefficients ( $\alpha$) are calculated as\+:

\[
   \Delta H_{obs} = -10 \times (N^*-1) + 7.53 \times (N^{*2/3}-1) - 0.1 \times 10 \quad (\mbox{kcal}\,\mbox{M}^{-1})
 \]  \[
   \Delta S_{obs} = -13 \times (N^*-1) - 19 \times (N^*-1) + 9.21 \times (N^{*2/3}-1) - 0.1 \times 13 \quad (\mbox{cal}\,\mbox{M}^{-1}\,\mbox{K}^{-1})
 \]  \[
   \frac{\alpha}{1-\alpha} = e^{\frac{-\Delta G^{*}}{RT}}
 \] If $\Delta H$ and $\Delta S$ are not provided, the mass accomodation coefficient is assumed to be 0.\+1 ({\bfseries [Zaveri2008]}).

Condensation rate constants are calculated following {\bfseries [Zaveri2008]} as\+:  \[
    k_c = 4 \pi r D_g f_{fs}( K_n, \alpha )
  \] where $r$ is the radius of the particle(s) \mbox{[}m\mbox{]}, $D_g$ is the diffusion coefficient of the gas-\/phase species \mbox{[} $\mbox{m}^2\,\mbox{s}^{-1}$\mbox{]}, $f_{fs}( K_n, \alpha )$ is the Fuchs Sutugin transition regime correction factor \mbox{[}unitless\mbox{]}, $K_n$ is the Knudsen Number \mbox{[}unitess\mbox{]}, and $\alpha$ is the mass accomodation coefficient.

Rates can be calculated as\+:  \[
    r_c = [G] N_a k_c
  \] where $[G]$ is the gas-\/phase species concentration \mbox{[}ppm\mbox{]}, $N_a$ is the number concentration of particles \mbox{[} $\mbox{particle}\,\mbox{m}^{-3}$\mbox{]} and the rate $r_c$ is in \mbox{[} $\mbox{ppm}\,\mbox{s}^{-1}$\mbox{]}. The particle radius used to calculate $k_{f}$ is the effective radius \mbox{[} $r_{eff}$\mbox{]}, which is taken as the "{}least-\/wrong"{} choice for condensation rates, as it is weighted to surface area {\bfseries [Zender2002]} .

 The key-\/value pairs {\bfseries{gas-\/phase}} {\bfseries{species}}, {\bfseries{aerosol}} {\bfseries{phase}} and {\bfseries{aerosol-\/phase}} {\bfseries{species}} are required. Only one gas-\/ and one aerosol-\/phase species are allowed per phase-\/transfer reaction. The key-\/value pair {\bfseries{aerosol-\/phase}} {\bfseries{activity}} {\bfseries{coefficient}} is optional. When it is included its value must be the name of an species of type {\ttfamily ACTIVITY\+\_\+\+COEFF} that is present in the specified aerosol phase. When it is not included, activity coefficients are assume to be 1.\+0.

Gas-\/phase species must include parameters named {\bfseries{diffusion}} {\bfseries{coeff}} {\bfseries{}}\mbox{[}{\bfseries{m2}} {\bfseries{s-\/1}}\mbox{]}, which specifies the diffusion coefficient in $\mbox{m}^2\,\mbox{s}^{-1}$, and {\bfseries{molecular}} {\bfseries{weight}} {\bfseries{}}\mbox{[}{\bfseries{kg}} {\bfseries{mol-\/1}}\mbox{]}, which specifies the molecular weight of the species in $\mbox{kg}\,\mbox{mol}^{-1}$. They may optionally include the parameter {\bfseries{N}} {\bfseries{star}}, which will be used to calculate th mass accomodation coefficient. When this parameter is not included, the mass accomodation coefficient is assumed to be 1.\+0.

The key-\/value pair {\bfseries{B}} is also required and must have a value of an array of exactly four members that specifies the SIMPOL parameters for the partitioning species. The {\bfseries{B}} parameters can be obtained by summing the contributions of each functional group present in the partitioning species to the overall $B_{n,i}$ for species $i$, such that\+:  \[
   B_{n,i} = \sum_{k} \nu_{k,i} B_{n,k} \forall n \in [1...4]
 \] where $\nu_{k,i}$ is the number of functional groups $k$ in species $i$ and the parameters $B_{n,k}$ for each functional group $k$ can be found in table 5 of Pankow and Asher (2008) {\bfseries [Pankow2008]}.
