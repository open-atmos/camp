\doxysubsection{CAMP\+: Surface (Hetergeneous) Reaction}
\hypertarget{camp_rxn_surface}{}\label{camp_rxn_surface}\index{CAMP: Surface (Hetergeneous) Reaction@{CAMP: Surface (Hetergeneous) Reaction}}
Surface reactions transform gas-\/phase species into gas-\/phase products according to a rate that is calculated based on the total exposed surface area of a condensed phase.

For surface reactions of the gas phase species X, the reaction rate is calculated assuming large particles (continuum regime) as\+:

\[
      r_{surface} = k_{surface}\mbox{[X]}
    \]

where $[\mbox{X}]$ is the gas-\/phase concentration of species X \mbox{[}ppm\mbox{]} and the rate constant $k_{surface}$ \mbox{[}1/s\mbox{]} is calculated as\+:

\[
    k_{surface} = \frac{4N_a \pi r^2_e}{\left(\frac{r_e}{D_g} + \frac{4}{v(T)\gamma}\right)}
    \]

where $N_a$ is the number concentration of particles \mbox{[}particles $\mbox{m}^{-3}$\mbox{]}, $r_e$ is the effective particle radius \mbox{[}m\mbox{]}, $D_g$ is the gas-\/phase diffusion coefficient of the reactant \mbox{[} $\mbox{m}^2\mbox{s}^{-1}$\mbox{]}, $\gamma$ is the reaction probability \mbox{[}unitless\mbox{]}, and v is the mean free speed of the gas-\/phase reactant\+:

\[
      v = \sqrt{\frac{8RT}{\pi MW}}
    \]

where R is the ideal gas constant \mbox{[}  $\mbox{J}\, \mbox{K}^{-1}\,
    \mbox{mol}^{-1}$\mbox{]}, T is temperature \mbox{[}K\mbox{]}, and MW is the molecular weight of the gas-\/phase reactant \mbox{[} $\mbox{kg}\, \mbox{mol}^{-1}$\mbox{]}

 The key-\/value pairs {\bfseries{gas-\/phase}} {\bfseries{reactant}}, {\bfseries{reaction}} {\bfseries{probability}}, and {\bfseries{aerosol-\/phase}} are required. Only one gas-\/phase reactant is allowed, but multiple products can be present. The key-\/value pair {\bfseries{yield}} for product species is optional and defaults to 1.\+0.

The gas-\/phase reactant species must include properties {\bfseries{diffusion}} {\bfseries{coeff}} {\bfseries{}}\mbox{[}{\bfseries{m2}} {\bfseries{s-\/1}}\mbox{]}, which specifies the diffusion coefficient in $\mbox{m}^2\,\mbox{s}^{-1}$, and {\bfseries{molecular}} {\bfseries{weight}} {\bfseries{}}\mbox{[}{\bfseries{kg}} {\bfseries{mol-\/1}}\mbox{]}, which specifies the molecular weight of the species in $\mbox{kg}\,\mbox{mol}^{-1}$.
